Water Activities

In & On the Ocean

🤿 No Cert Needed
Snorkeling

Koh Tao's shallow bays are packed with coral and fish just below the surface. Hin Wong Bay and Mango Bay are perfect for snorkeling — no experience required.

🕐 Half day 👨‍👩‍👧 All ages
🌙 Certified Divers
Night Diving

The reef transforms after dark. Hunting octopus, sleeping turtles, bioluminescent plankton — night diving on Koh Tao is one of the most magical experiences in diving.

🕐 2–3 hrs 🤿 OW required
🌊 No Cert Needed
Freediving

Experience the silence of the ocean on a single breath. Freediving is a completely different way to explore — pure, weightless, and deeply meditative. Beginner courses available.

🕐 1–2 days 🫁 Breath training
🚤 Explore
Island & Sunset Tour

Hop on a longtail boat and explore the hidden bays, snorkel spots, and viewpoints around Koh Tao and nearby Koh Nang Yuan. Best sunset views in the Gulf of Thailand.

🕐 Half day 📍 Koh Nang Yuan
🐢 Conservation
Turtle Spotting

Green sea turtles are resident on Koh Tao year-round. Dive or snorkel at Turtle Pinnacle, Hin Wong, or Shark Island for the best chances to swim alongside them.

📍 Multiple sites 🐢 Year-round
📸 Add-On
Underwater Photography

Koh Tao's crystal-clear water and colourful reef make it a photographer's dream. Ask us about underwater camera rental and photography tips during your dives.

📷 Camera rental 🌈 Best visibility Jan–Apr

Dive Sites

Koh Tao's Best Spots

Chumphon Pinnacle
18–36m · Advanced
Koh Tao's most famous site. Giant grouper, whale sharks (seasonal), and huge schools of fish around towering granite boulders.
Sail Rock
6–40m · All levels
A pinnacle rising from the seabed between Koh Tao and Koh Phangan. Famous for whale sharks and a dramatic chimney swim-through.
Southwest Pinnacle
6–33m · Intermediate
Soft corals, moray eels, batfish, and barracuda. One of the most colourful sites on Koh Tao, great for photography.
Shark Island
6–18m · All levels
Despite the name, it's the turtles that steal the show here. Easy diving with beautiful coral gardens and clear visibility.
Hin Wong Pinnacle
8–30m · Intermediate
Remote and rarely crowded. Excellent marine life diversity — a local favourite for experienced divers.
Japanese Gardens
2–12m · Beginner
Perfect for OW training and fun dives. Shallow, calm, and teeming with clownfish, pufferfish, and colourful corals.
