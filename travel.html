🚢 Travel Guide

Getting to
Koh Tao

Koh Tao is a small island in the Gulf of Thailand — no airport, but very easy to reach by ferry. Here's everything you need to know.

Routes

Choose Your Route

✈️🚌🚢
From Bangkok
Bangkok → Koh Tao
  • Fly Bangkok → Surat Thani or Chumphon (1 hr)
  • Transfer to ferry pier by bus or taxi
  • Ferry to Koh Tao (1.5–3 hrs depending on route)
~6–8 hrs total
🚌🚢
Via Chumphon
Chumphon Pier → Koh Tao
  • Lomprayah or Songserm ferry from Chumphon
  • High-speed catamaran (~1.5 hrs)
  • Night ferry also available (~6 hrs, overnight)
Most popular route
⛴️
Via Surat Thani
Surat Thani → Koh Tao
  • Ferry via Koh Samui and Koh Phangan
  • Lomprayah high-speed catamaran
  • Total ferry time ~3 hrs
~3 hrs ferry
🏝️⛴️
From Koh Samui / Koh Phangan
Island Hopping
  • Daily ferries from Koh Samui (~2 hrs)
  • Daily ferries from Koh Phangan (~1 hr)
  • Lomprayah and Songserm operate both routes
Easy island hop

Useful Info

What to Know

🎫 Ferry Companies

  • Lomprayah — fast catamaran, most reliable
  • Songserm — older but budget-friendly
  • Seatran Discovery — from Surat Thani
  • Book online in advance, especially peak season

📅 Best Time to Visit

  • Jan–Apr — best visibility, calm seas
  • May–Sep — good diving, fewer tourists
  • Oct–Dec — some rough weather, cheaper rates
  • Diving is year-round on Koh Tao

🏨 On the Island

  • No airport — ferry only
  • Main pier: Mae Haad (Koh Tao)
  • Taxis and motorbike taxis at the pier
  • Most dive schools are 5–15 min from pier

💡 Pro Tips

  • Book combo ticket: Bangkok → Koh Tao in one
  • Night train + ferry is cheapest option
  • Bring seasickness tablets if needed
  • Arrive a day before your course starts
